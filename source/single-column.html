This Month in Japan

The old name for the month of January, Mutsuki, means “month of harmony” or “month where friends and relatives gather.”

One of the most important national holidays in Japan is Oshogatsu (New Year's Day). Schools are typically closed for about 2 weeks, and many business are closed from December 28 through January 1. Read 14 Ways to Have a Happy New Year in Japan

Coming-of-Age Day, observed on the second Monday of January, is another national holiday this month. On this day, women often wear an elaborate style of kimono called furisode while men typically wear formal suits to celebrate being 20-years-old, the age of adulthood in Japan.

JETAA Shinnenkai New Year Celebration @ JIC

JETAA Chicago is proud to present the 13th annual Shinnenkai New Year’s Party to celebrate the Year of the Monkey!

Enjoy appetizers, snacks, beer, wine, and soft drinks. Experience Japan’s traditional arts and crafts including: calligraphy, origami, and kimono dress-up. Challenge your friends in a trivia tournament, a game of karuta (picture matching game), or $1 Janken (Rock, Paper, Scissors) Contest!

Win prizes in the charity raffle benefitting Smile Kids Japan. Prizes include gift certificates to local businesses, Japanese themed treats and treasures, and more!

Want to volunteer at the event? Volunteer shifts are 4:30-6 PM or 6-8:30 PM and are an easy way to get involved, meet people and save money! Find out more by emailing JETAA Chicago.

Illinois Japan Bowl Application Now Open!

This academic competition covers a wide range of topics that tests high school stuents who are studying the Japanese language across the state of Illinois. The competition tests not only their knowledge of the language, but also their understanding of traditional and modern Japan.

The participants make a team of 3 members and the champion team for Level 4 goes to compete in the National Bowl in Washington D.C. through the Mazda Foundation Grant.

Applications must be submitted by February 29, 2016 to the Japan American Society of Chicago. Read more details and find out how to apply here.
